* The Three Phases of AI-Assisted Coding

** Phase 1: Copy-Paste Era

#+begin_export latex
\begin{center}
\begin{tikzpicture}[scale=0.8, transform shape,
    node distance=1.4cm,
    box/.style={draw, rounded corners, minimum width=2.2cm, minimum height=0.8cm, align=center, fill=gray!10, font=\small},
    arr/.style={-{Stealth[length=2.5mm]}, thick}
  ]
  \node[box] (browser) {Browser\\ChatGPT};
  \node[box, right=of browser] (read) {Read\\Output};
  \node[box, right=of read] (copy) {Copy-Paste\\into Editor};
  \node[box, right=of copy] (fix) {Fix \& Pray};
  \draw[arr] (browser) -- (read);
  \draw[arr] (read) -- (copy);
  \draw[arr] (copy) -- (fix);
  \draw[arr, dashed, bend left=30] (fix) to node[above, font=\scriptsize] {repeat} (browser);
\end{tikzpicture}
\end{center}
#+end_export

- Open ChatGPT in browser, describe the problem
- Copy-paste code into editor, manually fix mismatches
- *No project awareness, no file access*

** Phase 2: CLI Tools --- Full Context

- AI reads your codebase, edits files directly
- You review and approve changes
- *Collaboration, not dictation*

** Phase 3: Agents --- The Final Frontier

#+begin_export latex
\begin{center}
\begin{tikzpicture}[scale=0.75, transform shape,
    node distance=1cm and 1.8cm,
    agent/.style={draw, rounded corners, minimum width=2.2cm, minimum height=0.8cm, align=center, fill=green!10, font=\small},
    action/.style={draw, rounded corners, minimum width=1.8cm, minimum height=0.6cm, align=center, fill=orange!10, font=\scriptsize},
    arr/.style={-{Stealth[length=2.5mm]}, thick}
  ]
  \node[agent] (you) {\textbf{You} (specify intent)};
  \node[agent, below=of you] (agent) {\textbf{AI Agent}};
  \node[action, below left=0.7cm and 0.3cm of agent] (code) {Write Code};
  \node[action, below=0.7cm of agent] (test) {Run Tests};
  \node[action, below right=0.7cm and 0.3cm of agent] (deploy) {Create PR};

  \draw[arr] (you) -- node[right, font=\scriptsize] {task} (agent);
  \draw[arr] (agent) -- (code);
  \draw[arr] (agent) -- (test);
  \draw[arr] (agent) -- (deploy);

  \draw[arr, dashed, bend left=40] (test.south) to node[below, font=\scriptsize] {fix \& retry} (code.south);
\end{tikzpicture}
\end{center}
#+end_export

- Deploy agents with a goal --- they plan, code, test autonomously
- You review the outcome, not every keystroke
- *Demo: session log from yesterday*

** The Real Win: Process Without the Pain

#+begin_export latex
\begin{center}
\begin{tikzpicture}[scale=0.75, transform shape,
    node distance=0.6cm,
    phase/.style={draw, rounded corners, minimum width=2.8cm, minimum height=0.7cm, align=center, font=\small},
    skip/.style={phase, fill=red!10, text=red!60!black},
    bot/.style={phase, fill=green!10, text=green!40!black},
    arr/.style={-{Stealth[length=2.5mm]}, thick}
  ]
  % SDLC phases
  \node[phase, fill=gray!10] (req) {Requirements};
  \node[phase, fill=gray!10, right=of req] (design) {Design};
  \node[phase, fill=gray!10, right=of design] (code) {Code};
  \node[phase, fill=gray!10, right=of code] (test) {Test};
  \node[phase, fill=gray!10, right=of test] (deploy) {Deploy};

  \draw[arr] (req) -- (design);
  \draw[arr] (design) -- (code);
  \draw[arr] (code) -- (test);
  \draw[arr] (test) -- (deploy);

  % "Skipped" overlay
  \node[skip, below=0.8cm of req] (s1) {Skipped};
  \node[skip, below=0.8cm of design] (s2) {Rushed};
  \node[skip, below=0.8cm of test] (s4) {Skipped};

  % Bot overlay
  \node[bot, below=0.8cm of s1] (b1) {Bot};
  \node[bot, below=0.8cm of s2] (b2) {Bot};
  \node[bot, below=0.8cm of s4] (b4) {Bot};

  % Labels
  \node[left=0.3cm of req, font=\footnotesize\bfseries] {SDLC};
  \node[left=0.3cm of s1, font=\footnotesize\bfseries, text=red!60!black] {Reality};
  \node[left=0.3cm of b1, font=\footnotesize\bfseries, text=green!40!black] {Now};
\end{tikzpicture}
\end{center}
#+end_export

- SDLC was the gold standard --- developers skipped the boring parts
- Quality, timelines, and budgets suffered
- Now bots handle process; people focus on what they enjoy
